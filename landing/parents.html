Why not just use Google Calendar?

Google Calendar is for appointments. This is for running your actual life.

Google Calendar is brilliant at showing what's already in your diary. But most of what makes a family week work isn't in the diary — it's the pile of undated tasks that need to happen somewhere. That's the gap.

Google / Apple Calendar

Your appointments, beautifully displayed.

  • Shows fixed events: meetings, pick-ups, appointments
  • No place for tasks without a time yet
  • Can't help you figure out where things fit
  • Looks organised — until Monday ambushes you

How it works

To-do list meets week calendar. Finally in one place.

1

Add everything to your to-do list

Your Life Inbox is your family to-do list. Food shop, dentist call, football kit, permission slip — add it all, no time or date needed. Everything in one place, out of your head.

2

See the whole week in the calendar

School pick-up, the GP appointment, that meeting — your fixed commitments are already there. You can see exactly which gaps exist and when things can actually happen.

3

Drag tasks from your list into the calendar

Food shop goes Wednesday morning. Bedding on Saturday. Football kit Tuesday night. Pick each task from your to-do list, drop it into the calendar slot. Move things when the week changes. It always does.
