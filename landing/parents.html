The invisible job

You're running a small operation — and it all lives in your head.

Who has swimming, when the form's due, what's for dinner, which appointment clashes with pick-up. None of it is hard on its own. But holding all of it, all at once, all the time — that's the exhausting part, and it almost always lands on one parent.

Put the whole week somewhere you can see it and the load lifts. You stop carrying the logistics in your head and start seeing them on a page — where clashes are obvious and a quiet Tuesday evening is a real, plannable thing.

How it helps

The whole household, one clear week.

Get it out of your head

Capture everything — school, clubs, appointments, meals, admin — in the Life Inbox the second you think of it, so nothing lives only in your memory.

Lay out the week

Drag it onto the days it happens. Pickups, lessons and the food shop all sit together, so the busy evenings are impossible to miss.

Make the week work

Spot a clash, move a task, free up a lift. The week stops being a guess held in your head and becomes a plan you can actually run.

Why not just use a calendar?

A calendar is for appointments. This is for running your actual life.

Google or Apple Calendar will happily hold the dentist at 10:30. But the food shop, the permission slip, the gift you keep forgetting? Those aren't appointments — they're the loose, shifting work of family life, and a calendar has nowhere to put them.

A shared family calendar

Perfect for fixed events everyone needs to see. Keep it — it does that job well.

A to-do list on the fridge

Captures the tasks, but never tells you whether there's actually time to do them this week.

Big Picture Planner

Holds both — the fixed and the floating — in one visual week, so you can see what genuinely fits around school and work.

Planning family life, explained.

The best planner for a parent shows the whole household's week at a glance and holds more than appointments — it needs room for school runs, clubs, meals, errands and the admin that keeps a family running. Big Picture Planner combines a Life Inbox, where you capture everything the moment you think of it, with a visual weekly calendar, so the commitments and the to-dos live in one picture.

Get it out of your head first. Capture every fixed event (school, work, clubs) and every loose task (appointments to book, forms to return, shopping) in one place, then lay them across a visual week. Seeing the whole week makes the busy evenings, the gaps for errands and the clashes obvious — so you can fix them before the week starts instead of at the school gate.

A shared family calendar is great for fixed appointments everyone needs to see. Big Picture Planner is for the parent running the logistics: it also holds the loose tasks and admin that never make it onto a calendar, and it lets you drag them into your real week so you can tell what actually fits.
